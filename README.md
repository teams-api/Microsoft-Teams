### Create and Manage Your Team

In Microsoft Teams, a “team” functions as a digital hub for your class, department, or project group. Setting it up properly from the beginning ensures smooth collaboration over time.

**To request a new team** (typically initiated by administrators):

* Submit the team name
* Include a short description
* Assign an owner or administrator

Once the team is active:

**Add Members:**

1. Navigate to the team and click "More options".
2. Select "Manage Team".
3. Click “Add Member”, search for users by name or email, then select “Done”.

You can define user roles such as **Owner**, **Member**, or **Guest** to control access and permissions effectively.

## Organize Content with Channels and Tabs

Microsoft Teams structures discussions and resources through **Channels**, while **Tabs** help centralize frequently used tools and content.

**Channels:**

Each team includes a default “General” channel. You can create additional channels to focus on specific subjects, units, or collaborative projects.

To add a new channel:

1. Click “More options” beside the team name.
2. Choose “Add channel”.
3. Enter the name, select its privacy level (Standard or Private), and add a brief description.
4. Click “Add”.

**Tabs:**

Located at the top of each channel, tabs offer quick access to key features:

* **Posts** – Organized conversation threads
* **Files** – Shared documents and directories
* **Class Notebook** – A collaborative OneNote notebook
* **Custom Tabs** – Integrate apps like Forms, Planner, or other third-party tools

This setup helps minimize distractions and keeps important materials within easy reach.

## Schedule and Host Virtual Meetings

With Microsoft Teams, it's easy to set up and run virtual classes, meetings, or discussions.

**Ways to schedule a meeting:**

* Go to the **Calendar** tab and click **+ New Meeting**
* In a **channel**, use **Schedule a meeting** below the message box
* Within a **chat**, click the calendar icon to arrange a meeting with participants

Fill out the following:

* Meeting title
* Date and time
* Choose a channel or enter participant emails
* Set recurrence (daily, weekly, etc.)

**To invite external guests**, simply enter their full email address—they’ll receive a join link via email.

**Before joining a meeting:**

* Check your camera and microphone settings
* Enable or disable video/audio as needed
* Use “Meeting Options” to configure roles, lobby settings, and more

**To join a meeting:**

* From your calendar: Click “Join” a few minutes before the meeting starts
* From a chat or channel: Click “Join” on the meeting card
* From email: Use the link provided

Guests can participate via a web browser, but signed-in users will benefit from the full feature set.

## Collaborate on Files and Assignments

Microsoft Teams goes beyond communication—it's a full-featured platform for teamwork and learning.

**Sharing Files:**

* Upload files from OneDrive, your computer, or another channel
* All shared files are stored in the **Files** tab for quick access
* Collaborate on Word, Excel, and PowerPoint files right within Teams

**Collaboration Tools:**

* Use **OneNote Class Notebook** for journaling, brainstorming, or shared notes
* Add **Planner** tabs for task tracking or **Forms** for surveys and quizzes

**Simultaneous editing** is supported, enabling students to work together in real time—perfect for group activities.

## Track Student Progress and Use Class Notebook

**Assignments and Grades:**

Microsoft Teams includes a built-in assignment system tailored for educators:

* Create, distribute, and collect assignments
* Add grading rubrics and category tags
* Provide feedback and monitor student submissions
* Grades are tracked in the dedicated **Grades tab**

Assignments can be scheduled ahead of time, reused across different classes, and filtered by student for personalized insights.

**Class Notebook:**

Each team includes an integrated **Class Notebook**, which consists of:

* **Collaboration Space** – A shared workspace for group activities
* **Content Library** – A read-only area for important resources and materials
* **Private Notebooks** – Individual sections for each student

The notebook supports digital ink, audio, math input, and embedded content, making it a versatile tool for digital learning.
